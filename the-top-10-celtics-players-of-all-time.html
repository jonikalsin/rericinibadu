The Top 10 Celtics Players of All Time

July 2024 Â· 4 minute read

The Boston Celtics are one of the most successful and storied franchises in NBA history. With a total of 17 championships, the Celtics have had their fair share of legendary players grace the court. From the early days of the franchise to the present, there have been numerous players who have left an indelible mark on the team's history. In this article, we will take a look at the top 10 Celtics players of all time.

1. Bill Russell

Bill Russell is widely considered one of the greatest basketball players of all time, and for good reason. During his 13-year career with the Celtics, Russell won an astonishing 11 championships, the most by any player in NBA history. Known for his defensive prowess and shot-blocking ability, Russell was the backbone of the Celtics' dynasty in the 1960s. His leadership and basketball IQ made him an invaluable asset to the team.

2. Larry Bird

Larry Bird is often mentioned in the same breath as Magic Johnson when discussing the greatest players of all time. Bird's shooting ability and basketball IQ were second to none. During his 13-year career with the Celtics, Bird won three championships and was a 12-time All-Star. He averaged an impressive 24.3 points, 10 rebounds, and 6.3 assists per game throughout his career.

3. John Havlicek

John Havlicek was a key member of the Celtics' dynasty in the 1960s and 1970s. Known for his relentless work ethic and versatility, Havlicek could play multiple positions and was a consistent scorer and rebounder. He won eight championships with the Celtics and was named to 13 All-Star teams during his career. Havlicek's impact on the game goes beyond the numbers, as he was known for his clutch plays and ability to come through in pressure situations.

4. Bob Cousy

Bob Cousy, also known as "The Houdini of the Hardwood," revolutionized the point guard position with his flashy style of play. Cousy was a magician with the ball, known for his behind-the-back passes and no-look assists. He won six championships with the Celtics and was a 13-time All-Star. Cousy's impact on the game and his influence on future generations of point guards cannot be overstated.

5. Paul Pierce

Paul Pierce, also known as "The Truth," is one of the most beloved players in Celtics history. He spent 15 seasons with the team and won a championship in 2008. Pierce was known for his scoring ability and clutch performances. He ranks second on the Celtics' all-time scoring list, trailing only John Havlicek. Pierce's loyalty and dedication to the franchise make him a fan favorite.

6. Kevin McHale

Kevin McHale was a dominant force in the paint during his 13 seasons with the Celtics. Known for his post moves and footwork, McHale was nearly unstoppable on offense. He won three championships with the Celtics and was a seven-time All-Star. McHale's impact on the game can still be seen today, as many young players try to emulate his post moves.

7. Sam Jones

Sam Jones was a key contributor to the Celtics' success in the 1960s. Known for his clutch performances and scoring ability, Jones won 10 championships with the team. He was an integral part of the Celtics' dynasty and played alongside Bill Russell for most of his career. Jones' scoring ability and ability to come through in pressure situations make him one of the greatest Celtics players of all time.

8. Robert Parish

Robert Parish, also known as "The Chief," was a dominant center during his time with the Celtics. He won three championships with the team and was a nine-time All-Star. Parish's longevity and durability were impressive, as he played 21 seasons in the NBA, with 14 of those seasons spent with the Celtics. His presence in the paint and shot-blocking ability made him a formidable opponent for any team.

9. Kevin Garnett

Kevin Garnett's intensity and passion for the game made him a fan favorite during his time with the Celtics. He was instrumental in bringing a championship to Boston in 2008 and was known for his defensive prowess and leadership. Garnett's impact on the team went beyond the numbers, as his intensity and competitive spirit rubbed off on his teammates.

10. Bill Sharman

Bill Sharman was a key member of the Celtics' championship teams in the 1950s. Known for his shooting ability and defensive prowess, Sharman was a seven-time All-Star and won four championships with the team. He was a versatile player who could score from anywhere on the court and was known for his clutch performances in big games.

The Boston Celtics have had a rich history filled with legendary players. From Bill Russell to Larry Bird, these players have left an indelible mark on the franchise and have been instrumental in its success. The top 10 Celtics players of all time have not only achieved individual success but have also contributed to the team's championship legacy.
