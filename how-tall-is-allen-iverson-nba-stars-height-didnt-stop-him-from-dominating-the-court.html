Bryant was a shooting guard, who spent his entire 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association (NBA).

In 2020, Bryant, along with his daughter Gianna and seven others, died in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California. 

The two Hall of Famers (Bryant and Iverson) famously met up in the 2001 NBA Finals, with the Los Angeles Lakers winning in five games.

According to Okayplayer, Bryant averaged 24.6 points per game, 7.8 rebounds per game, and 5.8 assists per game.

In August 2020, Iverson paid tribute to Bryant in The Player's Tribune, stating, "We might have grown up in different circumstances, but when I saw you on the court, and how hard you were going, I knew we were raised with the same mentality."

"I wasn't tall - but in my mind, I was going to be a giant out there every single night," he added.

In their 29 career events, Bryant and Iverson produced strong performances each time. Bryant and Iverson averaged 24 and 25 points per game, respectively, in those head-to-head encounters.

Of those games, Iverson's team won 13 and Kobe's Lakers won 16. In the 2001 Finals, Bryant defeated Iverson to win his second NBA title.
