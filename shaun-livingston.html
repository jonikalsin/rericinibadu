Shaun Livingston- Professional Career

Shaun began his professional basketball career in 2004 after being selected by the Los Angeles Clippers in the NBA draft. In terms of statistics, he achieved a career-best with 14 assists against the Golden State Warriors on February 23, 2007, during the 2005-06 season.

On February 26, 2007, he suffered a knee injury in a game against the Charlotte Bobcats, forcing him to miss several games. He signed a two-year contract with the Miami Heat in October 2008, but he returned to the Memphis Grizzlies on January 7, 2009.

Livingston signed with the Tulsa 66ers of the NBA D-League, which is owned by the Oklahoma City Thunder, on March 7, 2009. He signed a multi-year contract with the Thunder on March 31, 2009, but was fired on December 22, 2009.

On the same day, he agreed to terms with the Washington Wizards on a 10-day contract. He agreed to stay with them for the rest of the season once more. Then, on July 20, 2011, he agreed to terms with the Charlotte Bobcats on a two-year, $7 million contract. He was dealt with the Milwaukee Bucks on June 23, 2011. He was sent back to the Houston Rockets, but he was released before the season began.

On November 15, 2012, he resigned from the Wizards but was promptly released on December 23, 2012. On December 25, 2012, Livingston was signed by the Cleveland Cavaliers, and in his first game, he recorded two points, one rebound, and one assist. He re-signed with the Brooklyn Nets before joining the Golden State Warriors on July 11, 2013.

In addition, on July 11, 2014, he signed a three-year, $16 million contract with the Mavericks. The Warriors waived him on July 10, 2019. He became innominate during his free agency.

Then, on September 13, 2019, it was reported that he had retired from the NBA in a post, saying, “After 15 years in the NBA, I’m excited, sad, fortunate, and grateful all in one breath.” — He noted that it’s difficult to portray all of the emotions that go into pursuing your dreams in a caption. On September 15, 2020, the Golden State Warriors announced Livingston’s employment as director of player affairs and engagement.
