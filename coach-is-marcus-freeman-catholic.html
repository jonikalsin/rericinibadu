Marcus Freeman, age 35, is the new football head coach of the University of Notre Dame. 

The University of Notre Dame has found their new head coach of the Fighting Irish. It's none other than Marcus Freeman. 

Freeman, a former linebacker, played collegiately for Ohio State University. He appeared in a total of 51 games during his college career as a weak-side and strong-side linebacker. 

He started his professional football career with Chicago Bears in 2009. He then went on to play for several NFL teams like Buffalo Bills and Houston Texans before retiring in 2010. 

He retired from pro-football due to a heart condition. However, he started his career as a football coach soon after. 

In 2011, he joined Ken State as a linebacker and assistant coach. He moved on to the Cincinnati Bearcats football team in 2016.
